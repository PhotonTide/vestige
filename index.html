On origin

Before art there was music. At fifteen I was writing poetry over instrumentals, teaching myself keyboard and guitar obsessed trying to find a language for things I didn't yet have words for. Music gave me that. It still does. There's a particular state it puts me in. A sort of focused and unraveling at the same time, everything deliberate and nothing controlled. Flow, I think, is the closest word.

I stepped back from releasing music to go all in on visual art eventually, but the two were never really separate. Vestige is where they finally meet.

On the concept

Sound disappears. The moment it exists it's already leaving. A painting stays. A photograph stays. Music asks you to hold it and memory isn't reliable.

A vestige is what's left after something passes through. That's what this is. You play something, something real happens in the frequencies and the energy of it, and this system catches that and makes it permanent. Not an interpretation. The actual thing.

There's a figure at the center of every piece. Still. Waiting. The music is yours and the figure is you. What you play determines what forms around you.

On the figure

Most pieces start dark. The figure is there from the beginning but you can't see it yet — it lives as a void in the canvas and the more you play the more the painting fills in around it and the more you emerge. You define yourself through what you create.

Some pieces start in white. Rare ones. If you open this and the canvas is pale you got something different — the figure isn't hiding, it's already there. Present from the start. Those are uncommon for a reason.

And then there are the ones that dissolve. The figure is present but breaking apart at the edges, bleeding into the field, losing its boundary between self and everything around it. These are the most abstract, the most unsettled. Some people exist that way too.

All of these say something about being human.

On the process

Play. Pick your key, your scale, your sound. The system reads the frequencies sixty times a second — bass builds the sweeping arcs of color, mids trace the flowing lines, highs leave fine marks. Every note adds something. Every pause leaves space. Most importantly feel the music as it is a part of you.

Mint when it feels right. That's your Vestige.
